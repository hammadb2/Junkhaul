## 1. NATURE OF EMPLOYMENT

The Employee is hired as a junk removal crew member. Duties include but are not limited to:
- Picking up and operating a U-Haul truck for scheduled pickup routes
- Driving to customer locations to collect junk and unwanted items
- Loading and securing items in the truck safely
- Transporting items to the landfill, storage facility, or donation center
- Collecting payment from customers (cash or card)
- Taking photos of items, loaded truck, and drop-offs as required by the app
- Obtaining customer signatures for proof of service
- Uploading receipts for all company card transactions
- Maintaining the cleanliness and condition of the truck

The Employee acknowledges that this is a physical labour position that involves heavy lifting, loading, and unloading of furniture, appliances, construction debris, and other materials.

---

## 2. HOURS AND PAY

### 2.1 Per-Job Hourly Pay
The Employee is paid hourly, with time tracked per job:
- **Clock starts** when the crew begins driving to the pickup location
- **Clock stops** when the Employee hits "Complete Load" at the landfill or storage facility

### 2.2 Overtime
Hours exceeding 8 hours per day or 44 hours per week are paid at 1.5x the regular rate, in accordance with Alberta Employment Standards.

### 2.3 Pay Period
Pay is processed every two weeks. Direct deposit is made to the Employee's bank account on file.

### 2.4 Deductions
All statutory deductions (CPP, EI, federal and provincial income tax) will be withheld from the Employee's pay in accordance with CRA requirements.

---

## 3. SCHEDULING

The Employer operates on **Thursdays and Sundays**. The Employee will be scheduled in advance and notified through the employee app. The Employee is expected to be available on scheduled days unless prior arrangement has been made.

The Employer will assign:
- Which days the Employee works
- Who the Employee is paired with (crew partner)
- Who is the designated driver for the day
- The U-Haul pickup location for the truck

---

## 4. EMPLOYEE RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 Phone and Data Requirement
The Employee **must have a working mobile phone with an active data plan** at all times during scheduled shifts. The employee app requires internet access to:
- Receive job assignments and route updates
- Navigate to pickup locations
- Collect customer payments
- Take and upload photos
- Communicate with dispatch
- Clock in and out

If the Employee's phone is non-functional or lacks data, the Employee cannot perform their duties and may be sent home without pay for that shift.

### 4.2 Traffic Violations and Fines
The Employee is **personally responsible for any traffic tickets, parking violations, photo radar tickets, or fines** incurred while operating the company truck or any vehicle during scheduled shifts. These fines are the sole responsibility of the Employee, not the Employer. Any such fines will not be reimbursed by the Employer and may be deducted from the Employee's pay if charged to the company.

### 4.3 Company Card Usage
The Employee will be issued a company card for the following expenses only:
- U-Haul rental fees
- Fuel for the truck
- Landfill/dump fees
- Other pre-approved job-related expenses

The Employee **must upload a receipt for every single transaction** made with the company card. Any transaction without a corresponding receipt upload will be flagged and investigated. Repeated failure to provide receipts may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 4.4 Truck Care
The Employee is responsible for:
- Taking photos of the truck (bed, sides, any damage) at pickup and return
- Taking a dashboard photo at pickup and return (for km and fuel tracking)
- Returning the truck with the same fuel level as when picked up
- Reporting any damage immediately
- Keeping the truck clean and organized

### 4.5 Customer Interaction
The Employee must:
- Be professional and courteous to all customers
- Verify that the load matches what the customer described
- Collect payment before loading (cash or card link)
- Obtain customer signature and typed name as proof of service
- Not begin loading until payment is confirmed

---

## 5. DONATION AND STORAGE PROTOCOL

Donateable items are taken to the Employer's storage facility first (not directly to charity). The Employee must:
- Confirm which items are donateable at pickup (check condition)
- Take photos of items placed in storage
- Take photos showing how full the storage unit is
- Use the access code provided in the app to enter the storage facility
- Follow the app's guidance on when a donation run is recommended

---

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

The Employee agrees to keep confidential all customer information, business operations, pricing, routes, and proprietary information encountered during employment. This includes customer names, addresses, phone numbers, and payment information.

---

## 7. SAFETY

The Employee must:
- Wear appropriate clothing and footwear for physical labour
- Use proper lifting techniques to prevent injury
- Not operate the truck under the influence of drugs or alcohol
- Report any injuries immediately to the Employer
- Follow all traffic laws and speed limits
- Not use a mobile phone while driving (the passenger/secondary crew member handles app interactions)

---

## 8. TERMINATION

### 8.1 By the Employer
The Employer may terminate employment at any time for:
- Just cause (theft, dishonesty, safety violations, repeated no-shows, drug/alcohol use on the job)
- Without cause with notice or pay in lieu of notice as required by Alberta Employment Standards

### 8.2 By the Employee
The Employee may resign by providing a minimum of 2 weeks' written notice.

### 8.3 Return of Company Property
Upon termination, the Employee must return all company property including the company card, any keys, access codes, and equipment.

---

## 9. PROBATION PERIOD

The first 90 days of employment are a probation period. During this time, either party may terminate the employment relationship without notice or pay in lieu of notice.
